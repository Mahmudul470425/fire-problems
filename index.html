Definition of Fire

Fire is a chemical process known as combustion (a burning). The basic requirements are air (Oxygen) burnable materials (solid, liquid or gas) and ignition temperature of the material (heat).

Here is a report for us from NSC' s website:

  • home fire-related injury occurs every 47 minutes
  • One home fire-related death occurs every three hours and eight minutes
  • In 2021, 1,353,500 fires resulted in 3,800 civilian deaths and 14,700 injuries.

Types of Fire

There aer four main types of Fire exist in the world now. These are discussed below

  • Solid Fire:

    which occurs as result of combination of vegetable materials such as wood, paper cotton, grass, coal etc is known as solid fire.

  • Liquid Fire:

    Fire which involves materials such as petroleum, oil, greases, alcohol rubber, Tar, wax etc is known as liquid fire.

  • Electric Fire:

    It is that fire which involves electrically operated instruments and appliances is known as electric fire.

  • Metal Fire:

    metallic substances such as sodium, titanium, zirconium, or magnesium. Class D fires cannot be addressed with water
